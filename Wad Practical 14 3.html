Indian Air Force (Bhartiya Vayu Sena) & Other Forces

Header: Touch the Sky with Glory (Nabhah Sprsam Diptam)


The Indian Air Force (IAF) is the air arm of the Indian Armed Forces, responsible for securing Indian airspace, conducting strategic reconnaissance, and projecting air power. The IAF is the world's fourth-largest air force, utilizing cutting-edge aircraft and technology to ensure air dominance.
Air Superiority & Strike Power
  • Modern Fleet: The IAF operates a mix of advanced multi-role combat aircraft, including the Rafale, Sukhoi Su-30MKI, and the indigenous HAL Tejas.
  • Strategic Lift: Utilizes heavy-lift transport aircraft like the C-17 Globemaster III and C-130J Super Hercules for rapid troop and equipment deployment during emergencies.
  • Air Defence: Protected by sophisticated layered systems like the S-400 Triumf Surface-to-Air Missile system.
  • Garud Commando Force: The elite special operations unit of the IAF, specializing in airfield security, counter-terrorism, and search and rescue during hostile operations.
The Extended Security Matrix: Forces Under MHA
Apart from the three main services (MoD), India's internal security is maintained by various forces under the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA), known as the Central Armed Police Forces (CAPFs).
