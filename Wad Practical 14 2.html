Indian Navy (Bhartiya Nausena) & Coast Guard

Header: Guardians of the Blue Water


The Indian Navy is the maritime branch dedicated to safeguarding India's extensive 7,516 km coastline and protecting its maritime interests across the Indian Ocean Region (IOR). With a blue-water capability, the Navy is a key player in ensuring regional stability, anti-piracy operations, and strategic deterrence.

Naval Commands & Assets


  • Naval Commands: The Navy operates through three commands, headquartered at Mumbai (Western), Visakhapatnam (Eastern), and Kochi (Southern).
  • Key Capabilities:
    • Aircraft Carriers: Operates Indigenous Aircraft Carriers like INS Vikrant, projecting air power far from the mainland.
    • Submarine Fleet: Maintains a potent fleet of diesel-electric and nuclear-powered submarines (Arihant class) to complete India's nuclear triad (land, air, and sea-based deterrence).
    • Surface Combatants: Includes advanced Destroyers and Frigates equipped with supersonic missile systems like the BrahMos.
  • MARCOS (Marine Commandos): The elite special forces of the Indian Navy, specializing in maritime counter-terrorism, amphibious operations, and covert infiltration.
