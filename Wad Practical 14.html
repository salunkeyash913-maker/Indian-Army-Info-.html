The Indian Armed Forces

Hero Section: The Sentinel of the Nation


"Service Before Self" (Seva Paramo Dharmah)
The Indian Armed Forces stand as the supreme military organization of the Republic of India, charged with defending the nation's sovereignty, territorial integrity, and national interests against external threats. Comprising the Indian Army, the Indian Navy, and the Indian Air Force, this integrated force operates under the strategic command of the President of India, who is the Supreme Commander. Our strength lies in our jointmanship, professionalism, and unwavering dedication to the motto, Service Before Self.

Integrated Command & Capability


India is committed to modern warfare synergy. Key joint structures facilitate seamless operations:
  • Chief of Defence Staff (CDS): The principal military advisor to the Defence Minister and head of the Department of Military Affairs.
  • Integrated Theatre Commands (Planned): Future structures aimed at combining the resources of the Army, Navy, and Air Force under a single commander for specific geographical or strategic zones.
  • Andaman and Nicobar Command (ANC): India's first permanent tri-service command, vital for securing the strategic island chain.
