Indian Army (Bhartiya Thal Sena)

Header: Land Warfare & The Frontier Sentinel


The Indian Army is the largest and most strategically deployed component, responsible for the security of India's land borders, including the challenging terrains along the
Line of Control (LoC) with Pakistan and the Line of Actual Control (LAC) with China. Our forces are trained to operate across the spectrum of conflict, from high-altitude
warfare to counter-insurgency and large-scale mechanized combat.

Core Roles & Formations


  • Primary Role: Deter external aggression and ensure the territorial integrity of the nation.
  • Secondary Role: Aid civil authorities during natural calamities (HADR â€“ Humanitarian Assistance and Disaster Relief) and counter-insurgency operations.
  • Operational Structure: The Army is organized into seven commands (six operational and one training), each led by a Lieutenant General.
  • Key Fighting Arms:
    • Infantry: The backbone of the Army; the ultimate fighting force that holds ground.
    • Armoured Corps: Utilizes battle tanks (like the T-90 and Arjun) for offensive maneuvers.
    • Artillery: Provides crucial indirect fire support with systems like the K9 Vajra and various howitzers.

Special Forces & Modernization


The Parachute Regiment (Special Forces) is the Army's elite unit, trained for a variety of complex missions, including counter-terrorism, hostage rescue, and specialized direct action. The Army is continually modernizing its inventory, focusing on improved surveillance, unmanned systems, and network-centric warfare to maintain a strategic edge.
